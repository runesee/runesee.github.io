Peasant Rebellion

Peasant Rebellion is a spin-off of the classic Atari game Space Invaders. There are starving peasants approaching in waves trying to attack you by shooting arrows. The goal of the game is to stay alive as long as possible, killing peasants and getting the highest possible score. The game ends when the player loses all their lives. The player is then prompted if they would like to save their score to the global leaderboard, inspired by old arcade machines, which is accessible from the main menu.

This game was developed by me and my group members during a software architecture course last semester. As a part of the requirements, we had to thoroughly consider the architecture of the game before we started programming. The game was written in Kotlin using Android Studio, and it was the first time I developed a mobile application. During the project, I was mainly responsible for the UX and UI design. This project was a lot of fun, and we ended up with a game that was pretty fun to play (at least in my opinion). A quick summary of the game can be viewed on Youtube in the video below.

The Middle Kingdom of Egypt, as it stands around 2010 BC, has recently been reunified by Mentuhotep II. Threats from both east and south still remain.

Egypt is nothing without control of the Nile. Will you seek to expand your control southward, marching your armies upstreams to Kerma's borders? Or will you stand in solidarity, and fight off foreign invaders together?

While the vast Sahara offers protection from the west, the Nile delta remains difficult to defend. There are rumors from the east of a great empire. Without a regular standing army, only you can decide the fate of Egypt.

A storm is brewing in Mesopotamia. Elam has long quarreled for control on the southern banks of the Tigris and Eufrates.

While the vast Sumerian empire stretches from the Mediterranean basin to the Persian gulf, Elam has been pushed out of Susa. This great Elamite city laying under foreign control is a dagger in the side of our great leader Kindattu.

While they may have occupied the city, Sumer too was weakened by the strife. For the future of Elam, Kindattu must return this dagger to its Sumerian owners. This Goliat must be felled!

Developing this mod has been a great learning experience for me. I have become acquainted with the scripting language Lua, as well as the tools Paint.net and GIMP for editing bitmaps. After over a month of developing, I feel I now have a good understanding of the source code and what I need to know for the first major milestone, namely populating the in-game map with all the relevant countries of the ancient world.
